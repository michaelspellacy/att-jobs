FAQs

  1. How does the Recruiting process work?

    Our recruiting process involves three steps, starting with a campus or virtual interview with an AT&T recruiter, followed by a second video interview and concluding, for top candidates, with a company visit to our headquarters in Dallas, TX. The on-site visit provides an opportunity for candidates to experience the AT&T culture, meet with current program participants and determine overall fit for this unique career opportunity.

  2. How do I apply for the (HRLDP) Human Resources Leadership Development Program?

    The application period for the current year has closed. Candidates are encouraged to return to this page in September 2014 when we begin accepting applications for next year's program.

    AT&T is an equal opportunity employer. All qualified applicants will receive full and fair consideration for employment.

  3. Does HRLDP hire international students?

    The HRLDP accepts students who will not require sponsorship for an employment or work visa. Other career opportunities within AT&T may exist for international students.

  4. How are job rotations assigned?

    Job rotations are selected for participants based on evaluations identifying key strengths, previous experience and passion, with an emphasis on enhancing their Human Resources acumen and providing exposure to key areas of the HR organization.

  5. How will my performance be evaluated?

    Participants are formally evaluated by their supervisor three times during their initial assignment, and twice during each of the following rotations. Performance is measured in two facets: what was achieved (results) and how these results were achieved (leadership). Participants also receive feedback from the HRLDP staff and other mentors.

  6. What kind of development and training do HRLDPs receive?

    Participants are offered robust on-the-job experience complemented by formal leadership training development courses, knowledge share sessions and opportunities to contribute to strategic projects. Throughout each rotation, participants enhance their Human Resources and leadership skills, gain exposure to core HR operations, interact with executive leaders and receive frequent developmental feedback while establishing a future career track.

  7. What types of career options can I look forward to upon completion of the Human Resources Leadership Development Program?

    The HR Leadership Development Program prepares graduates for positions with increased levels of responsibility and challenge. Graduates are advised to pursue HR roles where they will have the opportunity to expand their Human Resources knowledge and manage teams, further developing their supervisory skills.

  8. What benefits does AT&T offer?

    AT&T offers a comprehensive suite of benefits, including health, vision and dental insurance; a 401(k) savings plan; paid vacations and holidays; and much more.
